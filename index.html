MEAT US IN THE BLOODY ROOM

Mechado is a popular Filipino dish made by stewing meat in a tomato- based sauce with cubed potatoes, carrots, and bell peppers.

M E C H A D O

Dinuguan is a savory Filipino stew made with pork offal and/or meat simmered in a rich, spicy dark gravy made with pig blood, garlic, chili, and vinegar.

TOP MENU

Lechon

Lechon is a dish in which an entire pig or piglet is stuffed, glazed, and slowly spit-roasted over coals. The crispy, golden brown skin that tops the tender meat that melts in one's mouth is the most prized part.

Afritada

Afritada is a traditional Filipino dish made of braised chicken, beef, or pork in tomato sauce with carrots, potatoes, and red and green bell peppers.

Sisig

Pork sisig is a Filipino dish made from pig's face and belly, as well as chicken liver, and is typically seasoned with calamansi, chopped onions, and chili peppers.

SAVOUR EVERY BITE

Filipino cuisine continues to astound people with its distinct taste, creativity, and diversity. It reflects the Spanish, Japanese, Chinese, Western, and Pacific Islander flavors that emerged during our many years of colonization. Filipino cuisine is comprised of a diversity of flavors, colors, and delicious aromas. Each flavor expresses itself without having to blend together, resulting in dishes with a salty, sweet, and tangy flavor. Filipino dishes are vibrantly colored with vegetables, seafood, lean meat, and other ingredients. Each meal is a full and colorful sensory experience, and it will undoubtedly bring a one-of-a-kind experience to the table.
